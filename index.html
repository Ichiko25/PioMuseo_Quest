ABOUT

Welcome to the Museo ni Dr. Pío Valenzuela, a sanctuary of history built upon the very ground where one of our nation's greatest healers and patriots once lived. Born in 1869 in the town of Polo, Dr. Valenzuela was a man of remarkable duality: a physician dedicated to saving lives and a revolutionary leader committed to the birth of a free Philippines. As a key member of the Katipunan and a trusted confidant of Andrés Bonifacio, his life was a whirlwind of secret missions, clandestine publications, and a relentless pursuit of sovereignty. When you walk through these halls, you aren't just looking at artifacts; you are tracing the footsteps of a man who transitioned from the frontlines of a revolution to the halls of public service as the first Mayor of Polo and Governor of Bulacan.

This museum, officially opened in 2023, is the fulfillment of a long-held vision to preserve the 'bahay na bato' (stone house) legacy of the Valenzuela family. Though the original structure was lost during the ravages of World War II, this meticulous reconstruction serves as a living tribute to his 86 years of selfless service. Accredited by the Department of Tourism, our mission is to ensure that the flame of Dr. Valenzuela’s heroism his 'buhay at paglilingkod' continues to inspire every Filipino who passes through our doors. We invite you to explore the corridors of the past and discover the heart of the man whose name our city proudly bears.

Dr. Pío Valenzuela (1869–1956)

Dr. Pío Valenzuela was born on July 11, 1869 in Polo, Bulacan (now Valenzuela City). He was a physician-patriot and one of the “Big Three” leaders of the Katipunan alongside Andrés Bonifacio and Emilio Jacinto. A medical student at the University of Santo Tomas, he joined the secret revolutionary movement, served in the Camara Negra (Black Chamber). His legacy is honored today as Polo was later renamed Valenzuela City after him.
